Research Experience

Exploring the unknown and pushing the boundaries of knowledge through research. Below are some of the projects I have worked on.

Total Artificial Heart

August 2016 - November 2018

  • Introduction to TAH: Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) are a leading cause of death worldwide. The Total Artificial Heart (TAH) was developed as a solution to replace failing biological hearts.
  • Historical Development: The first artificial heart, called the Army Artificial Heart Pump, was developed in the 1960s by researchers at Harry Diamond Laboratories.
  • TAH in the Market: Several artificial heart models exist today, including Jarvik 7, Bivacor, CFTAH, SAH, SynCardia, Carmat, Polvad, and Abiocor, each with unique designs and technologies.
  • Future Plans: Research is ongoing to create a cost-effective, durable, and biocompatible artificial heart using advanced materials like Titanium and Biomimetic Polymers.
  • Conclusion: Artificial hearts provide a promising solution for heart failure patients, but challenges remain in long-term effectiveness and affordability.

Design and Development of Low-Cost Transfemoral Prosthesis

February 2020 - March 2021

  • Project Overview: This final-year project focused on designing and developing a low-cost transfemoral prosthesis, ensuring both functionality and aesthetics for amputees.
  • Design & Fabrication: Following an extensive literature review, the prosthetic limb was designed using SOLIDWORKS. The components were 3D-printed and fabricated to create a fully functional mechanical prototype.
  • User-Centric Approach: Special emphasis was placed on comfort and psychological well-being, allowing amputees to use the prosthesis confidently without embarrassment.
  • Academic Contribution: The project resulted in a peer-reviewed conference paper titled "Design and Development of Low-Cost Transfemoral Prosthesis," which was presented at ICSTB-2021-BCSIR.
  • Startup Recognition: The project was selected as one of the top 20 startups in a national innovation competition, highlighting its potential for real-world application and commercialization.

Designing Flow-Diverter Stent for the Treatment of Cerebral Aneurysm

July 2020 - November 2020

  • Project Overview: This project focused on simulating the effect of a stent within a blocked passageway to evaluate its impact on fluid flow, using both pre-treatment and post-treatment stages.
  • Design & Simulation: An idealized geometry was designed using SOLIDWORKS, followed by detailed simulations using Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) with ANSYS to analyze the fluid dynamics with and without the stent in place.
  • Stent Overview: A stent is a small tube inserted into a blocked passageway to restore the flow of blood or other fluids. Stents are typically made of metal or plastic, while larger stents used for larger arteries are called stent grafts.
  • Academic Contribution: This project was part of an undergraduate assignment, aimed at providing insight into the role of stents in restoring fluid flow through blocked pathways.
  • Files Provided: The parasolid and STL files of the design have been shared as requested, contributing to further study or adaptation.
